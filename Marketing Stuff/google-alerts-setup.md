# Google Alerts & Brand Monitoring Setup — JMS Dev Lab

## Why This Matters

Setting up brand monitoring takes 15 minutes and runs forever on autopilot. It lets you:

- **Catch brand mentions** — Know when someone talks about your apps or company, positive or negative
- **Find unlinked mentions** — When someone mentions your product but doesn't link to you, that's a free backlink opportunity. Reach out and ask politely.
- **Monitor competitors** — See what competitors are doing, what people say about them, and where content gaps exist
- **Find content opportunities** — Discover what questions people are asking in your space, then write about those topics

### Settings for each alert:
- **How often:** "As-it-happens" for `"JMS Dev Lab"` (your primary brand — you want to know immediately). "Once a day" for everything else.
- **Sources:** Automatic (Google decides — covers news, blogs, web, discussions)
- **Language:** English
- **Region:** Any (your apps are used internationally)
- **How many:** "All results" (not just "best results")
- **Deliver to:** Your email address

### Notes on search terms:
- Quotes around multi-word terms force exact match — `"JMS Dev Lab"` won't match "JMS" or "Dev Lab" separately
- Adding qualifiers like `Shopify` or `jewelry` to common words (SmartCash, StaffHub, RepairDesk) reduces false positives from unrelated products with similar names
- `"Pitch Side" coaching` filters out unrelated uses of "pitch side" in cricket or baseball contexts

## What To Do When You Get an Alert

### Brand mention WITH a link to your site
- **Action:** Engage. Thank them if appropriate. Leave a thoughtful comment on their post. Share their content on your channels if it's genuine and helpful.
- **Don't:** Ignore it. Positive mentions are opportunities to build relationships.

### Brand mention WITHOUT a link to your site
- **Action:** This is a free backlink opportunity. Reach out to the author via email or contact form with a brief, polite message:

> Hi [name], thanks for mentioning [App Name] in your [post/article]. I noticed it doesn't include a link to the app — would you mind adding one? The URL is [URL]. Happy to help if you need any additional details for the piece. Thanks, John

- **Don't:** Be pushy or demanding. One polite email is enough. If they don't respond, move on.

### Competitor mention
- **Action:** Read the context. Note what people like and dislike about the competitor. Look for:
  - Complaints about the competitor (potential content: "Why [users] are looking for [Competitor] alternatives")
  - Features people wish the competitor had (potential feature ideas for your app)
  - Publications or blogs that cover your competitors (potential outreach targets for your own coverage)
- **Don't:** Leave comments trash-talking competitors. Stay above that.

### Industry topic mention
- **Action:** Evaluate the opportunity:
  - **Blog post or article:** Could you leave a helpful comment that links back to relevant content on your site?
  - **Forum discussion:** Could you provide a genuinely useful answer?
  - **News story:** Could you write a response piece on your own blog?
- **Don't:** Spam comments with links. Only engage if you can add genuine value to the conversation.

## HARO / Connectively

HARO (Help A Reporter Out) rebranded to **Connectively** (https://www.connectively.us/). It connects journalists with expert sources. Getting quoted in articles earns high-authority backlinks.

### How to sign up:
1. Go to https://www.connectively.us/
2. Sign up as a **Source** (free)
3. Select your areas of expertise

### Categories to monitor:
- **Business and Finance** — Small business, entrepreneurship, SaaS
- **Technology** — Software development, Shopify, e-commerce tools
- **Lifestyle** — Jewelry industry (if available)
- **General** — Scan for anything relevant to custom software, no-code tools, Irish business

### How to respond (written only):
- Journalists send out queries. You'll receive daily emails with requests.
- Scan for queries where you have genuine expertise:
  - Custom software costs
  - Shopify app development
  - Small business technology
  - Spreadsheet replacement
  - No-code vs custom software
  - Jewelry industry technology
  - Cashflow management for small business
  - Irish small business / tech startup scene
- **Respond quickly** — journalists work on tight deadlines. Same-day responses have the best chance.
- **Keep responses concise** — 2-4 paragraphs max. Answer the specific question asked.
- **Include your credentials:** "John Moore, founder of JMS Dev Lab, a custom software company based in Cork, Ireland. I've built 7 production apps for the Shopify platform."
- **Written responses only** — do not agree to phone calls, video interviews, or podcast appearances. If they ask for a follow-up call, offer to provide additional information via email instead.
- **Format template:**

> Hi [journalist name],
>
> Re: [query topic]
>
> [2-4 paragraphs answering their specific question with concrete details, not vague generalities]
>
> John Moore
> Founder, JMS Dev Lab (https://jmsdevlab.com)
> Custom software development, Cork, Ireland
> 7 production Shopify apps built and shipped

### What makes a good HARO response:
- Specific numbers and examples, not generic advice
- A unique angle (you're a solo developer who also owned a retail business — that's unusual)
- Quick turnaround (respond within hours, not days)
- Professional but conversational tone

## Quora

Quora is a long-tail SEO play. Well-written answers rank in Google for years and drive steady traffic.

### Types of questions to answer:

Look for questions like:
- "What's the best CRM for jewelry stores?"
- "How much does custom software cost for a small business?"
- "Should I replace my spreadsheet with custom software?"
- "What are the best Shopify apps for [category]?"
- "Is Airtable worth it for a small team?"
- "How do I manage cashflow in my Shopify store?"
- "What software do jewellers use for valuations?"
- "How do I track repairs in a jewelry shop?"
- "Is no-code better than custom development?"
- "How do I find a good developer for my small business?"

### How to write answers that link back naturally:

1. **Answer the question thoroughly first.** The answer should be useful even without any link. Give specific, actionable advice. Use your real experience.

2. **Mention your product only when it's genuinely relevant.** Don't force it. If the question is "What's the best CRM for jewelry stores?" then mentioning JewelryStudioManager is natural. If the question is about general small business tips, don't shoehorn in a product mention.

3. **Frame it as personal experience, not advertising.** Use phrases like:
   - "I built an app that does exactly this..."
   - "I ran a jewelry store for years, and the tool I ended up building..."
   - "When I faced this problem myself, I..."

4. **Link to a blog post, not a product page.** A link to "How Much Does Custom Software Cost?" is more helpful (and less salesy) than a link to your pricing page. Quora moderators are more likely to leave educational links in place.

5. **Keep the answer substantial.** 200-400 words minimum. Short answers with links look like spam. Long, detailed answers with a single relevant link look like expertise.

6. **Don't answer in batches.** Spread answers over time — a few per week. Answering 20 questions in one day looks like a spam campaign and may trigger Quora's moderation.

### Example answer structure:

> **Question:** How much does custom software cost for a small business?
>
> [2-3 paragraphs of genuine, detailed answer with specific numbers and examples from your experience]
>
> I've written a detailed breakdown of real project costs on my blog if you want the full picture: [link to blog post].
>
> [Final paragraph with additional context or advice]

## Maintenance

### Weekly (5 minutes):
- Scan Google Alert emails for anything requiring action
- Check Connectively/HARO digest for relevant queries

### Monthly (15 minutes):
- Answer 2-4 Quora questions
- Review competitor alerts for trends or content ideas
- Check if any unlinked mentions were found that need outreach

### Quarterly (30 minutes):
- Review which alerts are generating useful results
- Add new search terms if you've launched new products or content
- Remove alerts that only generate noise
- Check Quora answers are still visible and not moderated

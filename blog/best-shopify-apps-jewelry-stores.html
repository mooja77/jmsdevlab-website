Best Shopify Apps for Jewelry Stores in 2026

12 March 2026

Running a jewelry store on Shopify is different from running a clothing brand or a general retail shop. Your workflows are different. You deal with commissions for sales staff, insurance-grade valuation certificates, bespoke repair jobs that take weeks, and client relationships that span years. Generic Shopify apps weren't built for any of that.

Most jewelry store owners end up cobbling together a mix of spreadsheets, generic CRMs, and paper forms to fill the gaps. It works — until it doesn't. Missed commissions cause staff disputes. Handwritten repair tickets get lost. Valuation records live in a filing cabinet instead of being linked to the customer who needs them.

The good news: there are now Shopify apps built specifically for the jewelry trade. Here's what to look for and which ones are worth installing.

What Jewelry Stores Actually Need From Shopify Apps

Before jumping into specific tools, it helps to map out the operational gaps that most jewelry stores face on Shopify. These are the areas where generic apps consistently fall short:

  • Commission tracking — Jewelry sales staff often work on commission, and the structures can be complex. Tiered rates, split commissions on collaborative sales, bonuses for hitting monthly targets. A basic POS report won't cut it.
  • Client relationship management — A customer who bought an engagement ring will likely come back for wedding bands, anniversaries, and gifts. You need to track purchase history, preferences, ring sizes, and special dates — not just email addresses.
  • Valuation certificates — Insurance companies require properly formatted valuation documents. If you're issuing these manually in Word or PDF, you're spending hours on something that should take minutes.
  • Repair ticket management — Ring resizing, stone replacement, chain repairs, watch servicing. Each job has its own timeline, cost estimate, and approval workflow. You need a pipeline, not a to-do list.
  • Customer portals — Clients want to check on their repair status or view past valuations without having to ring the shop. A self-service portal reduces phone calls and builds trust.

With those requirements in mind, here are three apps purpose-built for jewelry stores on Shopify.

JewelryStudioManager — CRM for Custom Jewelry Studios

JewelryStudioManager is a Shopify app designed specifically for jewelry studios that handle custom orders, commissions-based sales, and long-term client relationships. It's not a generic CRM with a jewelry skin — it was built from the ground up for this industry.

The core of the app is its commission tracking engine. You can define commission structures per staff member — flat percentage, tiered rates based on monthly sales volume, or split commissions when multiple team members contribute to a sale. The system calculates everything automatically from your Shopify orders, so there's no end-of-month spreadsheet wrangling.

On the CRM side, JewelryStudioManager stores the details that actually matter for jewelry clients: ring sizes, metal preferences, anniversary dates, past purchases, and notes from consultations. When a returning customer walks in, your staff can pull up their full history in seconds. That's the kind of service that turns a one-time buyer into a lifetime client.

Key features include:

  • Flexible commission structures with automatic calculation from Shopify orders
  • Client profiles with jewelry-specific fields (ring sizes, preferences, key dates)
  • Sales performance dashboards for individual staff and the whole team
  • Custom order tracking from consultation through to delivery
  • Integration with your existing Shopify product catalogue and POS

Pricing starts at $9.99/month, which pays for itself the first time it catches a commission discrepancy that would have otherwise gone unnoticed.

Jewel Value — Professional Jewelry Valuations

Jewel Value handles the valuation side of the business — the part that most jewellers still do with Word templates and manual data entry. It generates professional, insurance-ready valuation certificates directly from your Shopify admin.

The problem with manual valuations is consistency and time. Each certificate needs accurate gemstone descriptions, metal specifications, weight, measurements, and a declared replacement value. Formatting has to meet insurance company standards. When you're producing dozens of these per week, the manual approach becomes a bottleneck.

Jewel Value standardises the process. You fill in the item details using structured fields — stone type, cut, clarity, carat weight, metal purity, setting style — and the app generates a properly formatted certificate. The output is professional, consistent, and ready to hand to the customer or send directly to their insurer.

Key features include:

  • Structured valuation forms with industry-standard fields for gemstones and metals
  • Professional PDF certificate generation with your store branding
  • Valuation history linked to customer profiles in Shopify
  • Support for revaluations — pull up the original, update the replacement value, and issue a new certificate in minutes
  • Digital record keeping that replaces paper filing cabinets

Pricing starts at $9.99/month. If your store issues valuations regularly, this removes a significant admin burden from your week. No more formatting headaches, no more inconsistent certificates, and no more digging through folders to find a valuation from two years ago.

RepairDesk — Repair Management for Jewellers & Watchmakers

RepairDesk tackles the repair and servicing workflow that most jewelry stores manage with paper tickets, whiteboards, or basic spreadsheets. If you handle ring resizing, stone setting, watch servicing, or any kind of repair work, this app replaces your manual tracking with a proper job pipeline.

Each repair job gets a ticket with all the relevant details: item description, photos, customer contact information, cost estimate, approval status, and expected completion date. Jobs move through a visual pipeline — from intake to assessment, approval, in-progress, quality check, and ready for collection. At any point, you can see exactly where every job stands.

The real time-saver is customer communication. RepairDesk can notify customers when their job moves to a new stage, so they know when their ring has been assessed, when work has started, and when it's ready to pick up. That eliminates the "is my repair ready yet?" phone calls that interrupt your workshop every day.

Key features include:

  • Visual repair pipeline with customisable stages
  • Job tickets with photos, estimates, and approval tracking
  • Automatic customer notifications at each stage
  • Repair history per customer — see every job you've done for them
  • Works alongside your Shopify store without disrupting existing workflows

Pricing starts at $9.99/month. For any store that handles more than a handful of repairs per week, the time saved on tracking and customer follow-ups alone makes it worthwhile.

How to Choose the Right Apps for Your Store

Not every jewelry store needs all three apps on day one. The right starting point depends on what kind of work dominates your business.

If you're primarily a retail jewelry store with sales staff: Start with JewelryStudioManager. Commission tracking is likely your biggest pain point, and the CRM features will help you build stronger client relationships. Add Jewel Value later if you issue valuations regularly.

If you're a valuation-heavy business: Start with Jewel Value. If valuations are a core part of your revenue — whether as a standalone service or bundled with sales — getting those certificates standardised and digital should be your first move.

If you run a repair workshop or watchmaker's shop: Start with RepairDesk. Repair tracking is operationally complex and easy to get wrong. A missed job or a lost ticket costs you both money and reputation.

If you do all of the above: Many jewelry stores combine retail sales, valuations, and repairs under one roof. In that case, consider rolling out one app at a time. Get your team comfortable with each tool before adding the next. All three apps work within Shopify's admin, so they complement each other without creating conflicts.

The common thread across all three is that they were built by developers who spent time understanding how jewelry businesses actually operate — not by teams that built a generic tool and assumed it would work for every industry.

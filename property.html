The Problems Every Growing Property Business Hits

You started with a few properties and a spreadsheet. It worked. Then the portfolio grew and the cracks started showing.

"

Rent tracking is a spreadsheet with colour-coded cells

Green for paid, yellow for late, red for overdue. It works when you have ten properties. At thirty, it's a liability. You can't see arrears trends, you can't generate reports for landlords automatically, and the formula that calculates total outstanding rent broke two months ago and nobody noticed.

"

Maintenance requests come in by email, phone, and text

A tenant emails about a leak. Another rings about a boiler. A third sends a text with a photo. There's no single place to track what's been reported, what's been assigned to a contractor, and what's been resolved. Things get missed, tenants get frustrated, and landlords ask why the response time is so slow.

"

Tenants ring every week asking the same questions

When is my lease up? Can I see my payment history? Has the maintenance request been actioned? Every phone call takes ten minutes. Multiply that by fifty tenants and you've lost days every month answering questions that a self-service portal would handle automatically.

"

RTB compliance paperwork is scattered everywhere

Registration dates, notice periods, deposit protection records, inspection reports. Some of it is in the filing cabinet, some in email, some in a folder on the desktop. When the RTB asks for documentation, pulling it together takes hours. A proper system would have it all in one place.

"

We can't tell landlords what their vacancy costs are

A property sits empty for six weeks between tenancies. What did that actually cost? Lost rent, advertising, cleaning, repairs, re-letting fees — when none of this is tracked systematically, you can't give landlords the financial picture they need. And they're asking for it more and more.

What We Build

Custom Tools for Property Management

Not generic SaaS designed for the American market. Not enterprise software with features you'll never use. Tools built around how Irish lettings agents and property managers actually work.

Property Dashboards

See your entire portfolio at a glance: occupancy rates, rent collection status, upcoming lease renewals, outstanding maintenance, and financial summaries. One screen instead of seven spreadsheets.

Tenant Portals

A branded login where tenants can view their lease details, check payment history, submit maintenance requests with photos, and download documents. Cuts phone calls dramatically and gives tenants the self-service they expect in 2026.

Rent Collection Trackers

Track payments across every property and tenant. See who's paid, who's late, and who's in arrears. Generate landlord statements automatically. No more colour-coded spreadsheets.

Maintenance Request Systems

Tenants submit requests through the portal. Each request is logged, assigned to a contractor, tracked through to completion, and documented with photos. Nothing falls through the cracks. Full audit trail for every issue.

Financial Reporting

Generate landlord reports showing rental income, expenses, maintenance costs, and vacancy losses. Give your landlords the financial visibility they need without manually assembling reports from three different sources every month.

Compliance Management

Track RTB registrations, BER certs, inspection schedules, and notice periods. Get reminders before deadlines hit. Keep all compliance documentation in one searchable, auditable system.

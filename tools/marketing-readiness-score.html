Marketing Readiness Score

Is your business actually ready to start marketing? Answer 8 quick questions to find out if your foundations are solid — or if you need to fix a few things first.

Your 2-Minute Marketing Readiness Assessment

Select the answer that best describes your current situation.

1 Can you clearly explain what you sell and who it's for in one sentence?
2 Do you have a website that accurately represents your business?
3 Do you know where your best customers come from?
4 Do you have any way to collect leads or enquiries online?
5 How would you describe your current marketing efforts?
6 Do you have a budget set aside for marketing?
7 Can you handle more customers right now without things breaking?
8 Do you know what makes you different from competitors?
Please answer all 8 questions before getting your score.

Your Personalised Recommendations

    What Ready Businesses Do

    Businesses that get the most from their marketing tend to share a few things in common: they can describe what they do in plain language, they know where their customers come from, and they have a simple way to capture interest online.

    They don't necessarily have big budgets or fancy strategies. They just have the basics in place first — a clear message, a decent website, and enough capacity to deliver. Once those foundations are solid, even small, consistent marketing efforts start to compound.

    The biggest mistake most small businesses make isn't choosing the wrong marketing channel. It's starting to market before they're ready to make a good first impression.
